Elementary CT Signals

Unit Step & Unit Impulse

Unit Step
$$u(t)=\begin{cases}1,&t\geq 0\\0,&t<0\end{cases}$$
Relationship
$$\delta(t)=\frac{du(t)}{dt},\quad u(t)=\int_{-\infty}^{t}\delta(\tau)\,d\tau$$

Dirac Delta (Unit Impulse) Properties

  • Sifting: $\displaystyle\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x(t)\delta(t-t_0)\,dt = x(t_0)$
  • Scaling: $\delta(at) = \dfrac{1}{|a|}\delta(t)$
  • Even: $\delta(-t) = \delta(t)$
  • Multiplication: $x(t)\delta(t-t_0) = x(t_0)\delta(t-t_0)$
  • Derivative (doublet): $\displaystyle\int x(t)\delta'(t-t_0)\,dt = -x'(t_0)$
  • Replication: $x(t)*\delta(t-t_0) = x(t-t_0)$

Unit Ramp

$$r(t)=\begin{cases}t,&t\geq 0\\0,&t<0\end{cases}= t\,u(t),\quad \frac{dr(t)}{dt}=u(t),\quad \frac{du(t)}{dt}=\delta(t)$$

Exponential & Sinusoidal Signals

Complex Exponential
$$x(t)=Ce^{st},\quad s=\sigma+j\omega$$ $$x(t)=Ce^{\sigma t}e^{j\omega t}$$
Euler's Formula
$$e^{j\omega t}=\cos\omega t + j\sin\omega t$$ $$\cos\omega t=\frac{e^{j\omega t}+e^{-j\omega t}}{2}$$ $$\sin\omega t=\frac{e^{j\omega t}-e^{-j\omega t}}{2j}$$

Sinc Function

$$\text{sinc}(t)=\frac{\sin(\pi t)}{\pi t},\quad \text{Sa}(x)=\frac{\sin x}{x}=\text{sinc}\!\left(\frac{x}{\pi}\right)$$

Elementary DT Signals

Unit Impulse (Kronecker delta)
$$\delta[n]=\begin{cases}1,&n=0\\0,&n\neq 0\end{cases}$$
Unit Step
$$u[n]=\begin{cases}1,&n\geq 0\\0,&n<0\end{cases}$$
Relationship
$$\delta[n]=u[n]-u[n-1]$$ $$u[n]=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\delta[n-k]=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{n}\delta[k]$$
Sifting (DT)
$$\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,\delta[n-n_0] = x[n_0]$$ $$x[n]=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}x[k]\,\delta[n-k]$$

DT Exponential

$$x[n]=C\alpha^n;\quad |\alpha|<1\Rightarrow\text{decaying},\quad |\alpha|>1\Rightarrow\text{growing}$$ $$\alpha=e^{j\omega_0}\Rightarrow x[n]=e^{j\omega_0 n}=\cos(\omega_0 n)+j\sin(\omega_0 n)$$ \text{Periodic iff }\omega_0/2\pi = M/N\text{ (rational)},\; N = \text{fundamental period}

Steps to Compute Convolution (Graphical)

  1. Express $h(\tau)$ from $h(t)$.
  2. Flip to get $h(-\tau)$.
  3. Shift by $t$: $h(t-\tau)$.
  4. Multiply $x(\tau)\cdot h(t-\tau)$ and integrate/sum over $\tau$.
  5. Repeat for each interval of $t$.

Key Convolution Results

$$e^{-at}u(t) * u(t) = \frac{1-e^{-at}}{a}u(t)$$

$$e^{-at}u(t)*e^{-bt}u(t)=\frac{e^{-at}-e^{-bt}}{b-a}u(t),\;a\neq b$$
Geometric Sum (DT)
$$\sum_{k=0}^{n}\alpha^k = \frac{1-\alpha^{n+1}}{1-\alpha},\quad \alpha\neq 1$$
DT: $a^n u[n] * u[n]$
$$=\frac{1-a^{n+1}}{1-a}u[n],\quad a\neq 1$$

LTI System Characterization

CT LTI: $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k\frac{d^k y}{dt^k} = \sum_{m=0}^{M}b_m\frac{d^m x}{dt^m}$

DT LTI: $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k\,y[n-k] = \sum_{m=0}^{M}b_m\,x[n-m]$

For LTI: $H(j\omega)$ or $H(s)$ = transfer function; $y=h*x \xrightarrow{\mathcal{F}} Y=H\cdot X$

Eigenfunction Property

$$\text{CT: }e^{st}\xrightarrow{LTI} H(s)\,e^{st}, \quad H(s)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}h(\tau)e^{-s\tau}\,d\tau$$ $$\text{DT: }z^n\xrightarrow{LTI} H(z)\,z^n,\quad H(z)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}h[n]z^{-n}$$

Continuous-Time Fourier Series (CTFS)

Synthesis & Analysis Equations

Synthesis (Inverse)
$$x(t) = \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}a_k\,e^{jk\omega_0 t},\quad \omega_0=\frac{2\pi}{T}$$
Analysis (Forward)
$$a_k = \frac{1}{T}\int_{T}x(t)\,e^{-jk\omega_0 t}\,dt$$

Trigonometric Form

$$x(t)=a_0+\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\left[A_k\cos(k\omega_0 t)+B_k\sin(k\omega_0 t)\right]$$ $$A_k=2\,\text{Re}\{a_k\},\quad B_k=-2\,\text{Im}\{a_k\},\quad a_0=\frac{1}{T}\int_T x(t)\,dt$$

Dirichlet Conditions (Convergence)

  1. $x(t)$ is absolutely integrable over one period.
  2. Finite number of maxima/minima in one period.
  3. Finite number of discontinuities in one period (each finite).
  4. At discontinuities, FS converges to average of left and right limits (Gibbs phenomenon ≈9%).

Laplace Transform

Definition

Bilateral (Two-sided) Laplace
$$X(s)=\mathcal{L}\{x(t)\}=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x(t)\,e^{-st}\,dt,\quad s=\sigma+j\omega$$
Unilateral (One-sided) Laplace
$$X(s)=\int_{0^-}^{\infty}x(t)\,e^{-st}\,dt$$
Inverse Laplace (Bromwich Integral)
$$x(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{X(s)\}=\frac{1}{2\pi j}\int_{\sigma-j\infty}^{\sigma+j\infty}X(s)\,e^{st}\,ds$$

Region of Convergence (ROC)

  • ROC is a vertical strip in the $s$-plane: $\sigma_1 < \text{Re}\{s\} < \sigma_2$
  • Right-sided signal: ROC is a right half-plane $\text{Re}\{s\} > \sigma_{max}$
  • Left-sided signal: ROC is a left half-plane $\text{Re}\{s\} < \sigma_{min}$
  • Two-sided signal: ROC is a vertical strip (may be empty)
  • Finite-duration: ROC is entire $s$-plane (except $s=0$ or $|s|=\infty$ possibly)
  • ROC contains no poles
  • Causal & stable: All poles in left half-plane AND ROC includes $j\omega$-axis

Partial Fraction Expansion (Inverse Laplace)

$$X(s)=\frac{N(s)}{D(s)}=\sum_{k=1}^{N}\frac{A_k}{s-p_k}\quad\text{(simple poles)}$$ $$A_k = \left.(s-p_k)X(s)\right|_{s=p_k}$$ $$\text{Repeated pole of order }r:\; X(s)=\frac{A_{1}}{(s-p)^r}+\frac{A_{2}}{(s-p)^{r-1}}+\cdots, \quad A_k = \frac{1}{(r-k)!}\frac{d^{r-k}}{ds^{r-k}}\left[(s-p)^r X(s)\right]_{s=p}$$

Transfer Function & Stability

$$H(s)=\frac{Y(s)}{X(s)}=\frac{\sum_{m=0}^{M}b_m s^m}{\sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k s^k}$$
  • BIBO Stable: All poles of $H(s)$ have $\text{Re}\{p_k\}<0$ (strictly LHP)
  • Causal: ROC is $\text{Re}\{s\}>\max(\text{Re}\{p_k\})$
  • Causally stable: ROC includes $j\omega$-axis
  • $H(j\omega) = H(s)|_{s=j\omega}$ (only if $j\omega$-axis in ROC)

Sampling Theorem

Ideal Sampling (Impulse Train)

Sampled Signal
$$x_s(t)=x(t)\cdot p(t),\quad p(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}\delta(t-nT_s)$$ $$x_s(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x(nT_s)\,\delta(t-nT_s)$$
Spectrum of Sampled Signal
$$X_s(j\omega)=\frac{1}{T_s}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}X(j(\omega-k\omega_s))$$ $$\omega_s=\frac{2\pi}{T_s}=2\pi f_s$$

Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem

A band-limited signal $x(t)$ with $X(j\omega)=0$ for $|\omega|>\omega_M$ can be perfectly reconstructed from its samples if:

$$\omega_s \geq 2\omega_M \quad\Leftrightarrow\quad f_s \geq 2f_M \quad\Leftrightarrow\quad T_s \leq \frac{1}{2f_M}$$

Nyquist rate: $f_{Nyq} = 2f_M$

Nyquist interval: $T_{Nyq} = \dfrac{1}{2f_M}$

Reconstruction (Ideal Interpolation)

$$x(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,\text{sinc}\!\left(\frac{t-nT_s}{T_s}\right) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x(nT_s)\frac{\sin(\pi(t-nT_s)/T_s)}{\pi(t-nT_s)/T_s}$$ \text{Reconstruction filter: ideal LPF with } H_r(j\omega)=T_s,\;|\omega|<\omega_s/2

Aliasing

  • Occurs when $\omega_s < 2\omega_M$: spectral replicas overlap.
  • High-frequency components appear as low-frequency aliases.
  • Anti-aliasing filter: LPF with cutoff $\omega_s/2$ applied before sampling.
  • Alias frequency: $f_{alias} = |f - nf_s|$ for the appropriate integer $n$.

Discrete-Time Fourier Transform (DTFT)

Transform Pair

Forward DTFT
$$X(e^{j\omega})=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,e^{-j\omega n}$$
Inverse DTFT
$$x[n]=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}X(e^{j\omega})\,e^{j\omega n}\,d\omega$$
$X(e^{j\omega})$ is always $2\pi$-periodic in $\omega$. Fundamental range: $\omega\in[-\pi,\pi]$ or $[0,2\pi)$.

Z-Transform

Definition

Bilateral Z-Transform
$$X(z)=\mathcal{Z}\{x[n]\}=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}x[n]\,z^{-n},\quad z\in\mathbb{C}$$
Inverse Z-Transform
$$x[n]=\frac{1}{2\pi j}\oint_C X(z)\,z^{n-1}\,dz$$
Relation to DTFT: $X(e^{j\omega}) = X(z)\big|_{z=e^{j\omega}}$ (if unit circle is in ROC)

Region of Convergence (ROC) for Z-Transform

  • ROC is an annulus: $r_1 < |z| < r_2$ in the $z$-plane
  • Right-sided (causal): ROC is $|z|>r_{max}$ (outside largest pole ring)
  • Left-sided: ROC is $|z|<r_{min}$ (inside smallest pole ring)
  • Two-sided: ROC is an annulus $r_1<|z|<r_2$
  • Finite duration: Entire $z$-plane except possibly $z=0$ or $z=\infty$
  • ROC contains no poles
  • Stable causal: ROC includes unit circle $|z|=1$

Inverse Z-Transform Methods

1. Partial Fraction Expansion

$$X(z) = \frac{N(z)}{D(z)} = \sum_k \frac{A_k}{1-p_k z^{-1}}\quad\text{(simple poles)}$$ $$A_k = (1-p_k z^{-1})X(z)\big|_{z=p_k}$$ \text{If ROC: }|z|>|p_k|\Rightarrow p_k^n u[n];\quad |z|<|p_k|\Rightarrow -p_k^n u[-n-1]

2. Power Series (Long Division)

$$X(z)=\sum_{n}x[n]z^{-n}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\text{coefficients of }z^{-n}\text{ give }x[n]$$ \text{Divide numerator by denominator in powers of }z^{-1}

3. Contour Integration

$$x[n]=\sum_k\text{Res}\left[X(z)z^{n-1},\,z=p_k\right]$$ \text{Simple pole: }\text{Res}[X(z)z^{n-1},p_k]=(z-p_k)X(z)z^{n-1}\big|_{z=p_k}

Transfer Function & Stability

$$H(z)=\frac{Y(z)}{X(z)}=\frac{\sum_{m=0}^{M}b_m\,z^{-m}}{\sum_{k=0}^{N}a_k\,z^{-k}}$$
  • Causal & Stable: All poles inside unit circle $|p_k|<1$ AND ROC $|z|>r_{max}$ includes unit circle
  • DTFT ↔ Z-Transform: $H(e^{j\omega})=H(z)\big|_{z=e^{j\omega}}$
  • Zeros on unit circle → frequency nulls; poles near unit circle → resonances

Direct Form Implementations

For $H(z)=\dfrac{\sum_m b_m z^{-m}}{\sum_k a_k z^{-k}}$:

Direct Form I: Two separate delay chains (numerator then denominator). Uses $M+N$ delay elements.

Direct Form II (Canonical): Combine the two into shared delay chain. Uses only $\max(M,N)$ delay elements. More efficient.

Direct Form II Transposed: Reverse signal flow of Direct Form II.

Pole-Zero Analysis

$$H(z)=\frac{b_0}{a_0}\cdot\frac{\prod_{m=1}^{M}(1-c_m z^{-1})}{\prod_{k=1}^{N}(1-d_k z^{-1})}$$ $$|H(e^{j\omega})|=\frac{|b_0/a_0|\cdot\prod_m|e^{j\omega}-c_m|}{\prod_k|e^{j\omega}-d_k|}\quad\text{(geometric interpretation)}$$

Butterworth Filter

$$|H_N(j\Omega)|^2=\frac{1}{1+\left(\Omega/\Omega_c\right)^{2N}}$$ $$N = \frac{\log\sqrt{(10^{0.1A_s}-1)/(10^{0.1R_p}-1)}}{\log(\Omega_s/\Omega_p)}$$ $$\text{Poles: }p_k = \Omega_c\,e^{j\pi(2k+N-1)/(2N)},\quad k=1,\ldots,N$$
  • Maximally flat magnitude (all derivatives of $|H|^2$ zero at $\Omega=0$)
  • Monotonically decreasing magnitude
  • $-3\text{ dB}$ at $\Omega=\Omega_c$
  • Roll-off: $-20N$ dB/decade or $-6N$ dB/octave

Chebyshev Filters

$$|H_N(j\Omega)|^2=\frac{1}{1+\epsilon^2 T_N^2(\Omega/\Omega_p)}\quad\text{(Type I)}$$ $$T_N(x)=\begin{cases}\cos(N\cos^{-1}x),&|x|\leq 1\\\cosh(N\cosh^{-1}x),&|x|>1\end{cases}$$
  • Type I: Equal ripple in passband, monotonic in stopband
  • Type II (Inverse): Monotonic in passband, equal ripple in stopband
  • Steeper roll-off than Butterworth for same order
  • $\epsilon = \sqrt{10^{0.1R_p}-1}$

Duality Relations

  • CT: CTFS coefficients $\xrightarrow{\text{periodic extension}}$ CTFT samples; CTFT$\{$periodic signal$\}$ = impulse train
  • CTFS ↔ DTFT: FS coefficients $a_k$ are samples of $X(j\omega)/T$ at $\omega=k\omega_0$
  • Sampling ↔ Periodicity: Sampling in time $\Leftrightarrow$ periodicity in frequency; sampling in frequency $\Leftrightarrow$ periodicity in time (DFT)
  • DTFT ↔ DTFS: $X[k]=X(e^{j\omega})|_{\omega=2\pi k/N}$ (DFT as samples of DTFT)
